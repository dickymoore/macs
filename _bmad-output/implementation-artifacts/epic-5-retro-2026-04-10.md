# Epic 5 Retrospective

Date: 2026-04-10
Epic: 5
Epic Title: Recover from Degradation Without Losing Ownership Truth
Status: complete

## Epic Review

Epic 5 finished with all four stories complete and accepted:

- `5.1` classified worker health and surfaced warnings promptly.
- `5.2` froze risky work through intervention hold and lease suspension.
- `5.3` reconciled ambiguous ownership and rerouted safely.
- `5.4` resumed interrupted recovery from persisted recovery runs.

The sequencing was correct. Health classification came first, then risk freeze, then safe reroute, then persisted interrupted recovery. That order kept the controller from automating away ambiguity before it had the evidence and state model to do so safely.

## What Worked

- Health classification established a common vocabulary for degraded, risky, and blocked states before heavier intervention logic was added.
- Intervention hold and live-lease suspension preserved ownership truth while stopping unsafe progress.
- Explicit reroute and recovery-run handling kept ambiguous ownership from degrading into silent reassignment.
- Persisting interrupted recovery state made restart behavior more trustworthy and bounded later recovery work.

## Friction and Risks

- Intervention rationale and operator decision rights were still not first-class across all recovery paths.
- Audit evidence existed in pieces, but the project still lacked one durable event trail and unified history readers.
- Governance and policy boundaries were becoming important, but the repo-local configuration model was not yet explicit.

## Follow-Through From Previous Retrospectives

Epic 4 carry-forward items were completed:

- Health evidence became controller-correlated and inspectable in Story `5.1`.
- Task-scoped, lease-aware recovery behavior arrived across Stories `5.2` through `5.4`.
- Recovery work stayed on controller-owned seams instead of introducing worker-led reconciliation.
