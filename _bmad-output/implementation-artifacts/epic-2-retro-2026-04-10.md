# Epic 2 Retrospective

Date: 2026-04-10
Epic: 2
Epic Title: Govern Heterogeneous Workers Through Explicit Adapters
Status: complete

## Epic Review

Epic 2 finished with all five stories complete and accepted:

- `2.1` registered and inspected tmux-backed workers.
- `2.2` implemented the shared adapter contract and evidence envelope.
- `2.3` delivered the Codex adapter as the reference worker.
- `2.4` delivered Claude and Gemini adapters as governed workers.
- `2.5` delivered the local adapter and first-class qualification checks.

The epic landed in the right order. Worker registration came first, then the shared contract, then runtime-specific adapters, then qualification hardening. That let new runtimes extend controller-owned rules instead of inventing their own lifecycle logic.

## What Worked

- A single shared adapter contract kept runtime differences bounded and inspectable.
- Using Codex as the reference worker created a stable implementation target before adding more variable runtimes.
- Qualification checks prevented capability declarations from becoming implicit trust.
- The local adapter completed the minimum governed runtime set without weakening the controller-first model.

## Friction and Risks

- Work could now be assigned to workers, but routing policy and assignment rationale were still implicit.
- Protected surfaces and lock ownership were not yet explicit, which left conflict handling too manual.
- The controller could discover and qualify workers, but duplicate ownership claims and collision scenarios were still future hardening work.

## Follow-Through From Previous Retrospectives

Epic 1 carry-forward items were completed:

- Worker registration and inspection landed in Story `2.1`.
- The shared contract and evidence model arrived in Stories `2.2` through `2.5`.
