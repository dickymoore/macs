# Epic 1 Retrospective

Date: 2026-04-10
Epic: 1
Epic Title: Stand Up a Trustworthy Orchestration Session
Status: complete

## Epic Review

Epic 1 finished with all four stories complete and accepted:

- `1.1` started a single-controller orchestration session.
- `1.2` persisted authoritative control-plane entities.
- `1.3` enforced task and lease state invariants.
- `1.4` restored controller state safely on restart.

The sequencing was correct. Session bootstrap and persistence came first, then invariant enforcement, then restart safety. That established controller-owned truth before worker integration, routing policy, or operator ergonomics were layered on top.

## What Worked

- Controller-owned state and repo-local persistence landed before any heterogeneous runtime work, which kept authority boundaries clear.
- Task and lease invariants were defined early enough to shape later stories instead of being retrofit after adapter work.
- Restart recovery was introduced while the state model was still small, which reduced ambiguity around ownership restoration.
- The epic stayed narrow and foundational instead of mixing bootstrap work with later routing or intervention features.

## Friction and Risks

- Operator-facing inspect and lifecycle surfaces were still thin at this stage, which limited day-to-day usability even though the state layer was credible.
- The invariant model had not yet been exercised against heterogeneous runtimes, adapter drift, or conflicting worker claims.
- Restart safety existed, but health evidence, degraded-state guidance, and intervention controls were still future work.

## Follow-Through From Previous Retrospectives

This retrospective was deferred until project closeout, so Epic 1 had no earlier formal carry-forward items.

## Next Epic Preparation
