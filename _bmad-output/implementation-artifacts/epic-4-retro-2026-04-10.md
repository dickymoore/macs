# Epic 4 Retrospective

Date: 2026-04-10
Epic: 4
Epic Title: Operate Tasks from a Controller-First Surface
Status: complete

## Epic Review

Epic 4 finished with all four stories complete and accepted:

- `4.1` provided compact list and inspect commands for control-plane objects.
- `4.2` assigned and managed task lifecycle actions from one command path.
- `4.3` inspected degraded evidence and opened the right worker pane from context.
- `4.4` supported in-place pause controls and terminal accessibility modes.

The epic landed in the right order. Read-side inspection came first, then lifecycle actions, then degraded-context navigation, then pause and accessibility polish. That sequence let the operator surface grow around existing controller truth instead of duplicating it.

## What Worked

- Compact inspect and lifecycle commands made the control plane usable without weakening controller ownership.
- Degraded evidence and pane-targeting reused persisted controller context instead of relying on operator memory.
- Pause and resume support fit the existing lease and task model cleanly.
- Accessibility work arrived on the real command path instead of as a separate compatibility layer.

## Friction and Risks

- Recovery behavior for degraded or risky work was still limited, even though the operator surface was much better.
- Human-readable output still tended to lag state and JSON changes, which remained a QA risk.
- Worker health classification and intervention-hold flows were not yet first-class, so operators still needed too much judgment during instability.

## Follow-Through From Previous Retrospectives

Epic 3 carry-forward items were completed:

- Controller-first list and inspect surfaces landed in Story `4.1`.
- Lifecycle actions were consolidated in Story `4.2`.
- Context-rich degraded inspection and pause or accessibility controls arrived in Stories `4.3` and `4.4`.
